What it actually is

Think of it as a knowledgeable friend who knows every xTool machine. It plugs into the AI assistant you already chat with and gives it real answers about your laser: how big it cuts, which software it uses, tested starting settings for your material, how to fix a bad cut, and whether a material is even safe to burn. It's free, and it's built by makers for makers.

Under the hood: the tool is called an MCP (Model Context Protocol) server — a standard way to hand an AI assistant a specialized skill. You don't need to understand any of that to use it. It is not made by xTool; treat everything as a smart starting point, and follow xTool's official guidance whenever they differ.

Why it helps at the bench

Stop wasting material

Get power, speed, passes and DPI starting points for your exact machine and material — each with a confidence rating, not a wild guess.

Know your machine

Work area, laser type and power, and whether you use XCS or Studio — looked up for all 14 current xTool machines, not remembered wrong.

Fix problems fast

Describe the symptom — “not cutting through,” “smoky engraving,” “won't connect” — and get likely causes and fixes in the order to try them.

Stay safe

Instant “is this safe to cut?” checks. It flatly refuses dangerous materials like PVC and vinyl — and tells you why.

Make things quicker

Generates cut-ready files for you: material test grids, finger-joint boxes, and turning an SVG into an .xcs project.

Honest by design

When it isn't sure, it says so — and points you to a quick test grid instead of inventing a number. Every setting says “test on scrap first.”

Safety is non-negotiable
  • Test on scrap first. Every setting is a starting point, not a promise — always dial it in on a spare piece.
  • Never cut PVC, vinyl, or faux leather. They release chlorine gas that harms you and ruins your machine.
  • Never run a laser unattended. Stay with the machine, keep a fire extinguisher nearby.
  • Ventilate and protect your eyes. Use fume extraction and the right eye protection; use air assist where available.
  • Check generated files before running. Open any .xcs in Studio/XCS and confirm the machine, placement, and parameters.
  • When in doubt, follow xTool. If this guidance and xTool's official docs disagree, xTool wins.
